October 2020
Blake C Pfaff Arizona Stumo

Meet the Texas Fam!

You may be wondering, where am I living since my parents moved to Tempe? Let me introduce y'all to the amazing family that has taken me in during this time and made me part of their family.

The Martin's have been family friends since I was in high school. Gharron, my roommate, is in high school and is learning what it looks like to be a man of God! Micah (in the yellow) went off to college at OBU in Arkansas, and Abby is support raising as well working for FCA. 

Ministry in Texas

I know that God has me here in Texas for specific people in the short amount of time I am here. My favorite book in the Bible is Acts, and I look at Paul as a great example of being faithful wherever you are at no matter the time (Acts 28).

One of the funnest ways I have gotten to meet guys is through sports. I've gotten to be very good friends with two guys in particular, Saleem and Mike. I've been able to go hoop with Saleem (light grey) and all of his friends from the mosque and we have talked about the Bible, Christianity, and Islam.
I've been playing pickup football with Mike and we've been talking about what it look like to honor God while dating. Please pray for both of these men, I care for them deeply.

The most challenging yet rewarding ministry has been the discipling of my roommate, now little brother, Gharron. The Martin's have been pouring into him for over a year and love him so well. With G and I sharing a room, he sees me 24/7, day in and day out. If I skip getting in the Word or talk about others a certain way, he will pick up on that. The good thing is he is also picking up the great habits and disciplines we have as a family, and his love for God grows every month.

Please be praying for Saleem, Mike, Gharron, and me!

Support Raising Season

How am I able to do disciple and evangelize the students at Arizona full time? Only through the giving of others. Before I can begin ministering on campus, I must raise enough monthly support. My days right now consist of lots of prayer, phone calls, and meetings where I share what I'll be doing.

My common prayer in this season has been "God let me be faithful in my task, and trust you with yours". God is the provider of all things, and I trust that He will provide for me. I just need to be faithful in my task, which is making the phone calls, meeting, and asking.

I have been so encouraged by so many friends who have been praying for me during this time. I am always so grateful, and let me know how I can be praying for you and I will!
